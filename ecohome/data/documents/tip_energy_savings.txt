Everyday Energy-Saving Tips for Smart Homes

LIGHTING & ELECTRICITY MANAGEMENT:
- Turn off lights when not in use or use motion sensors in less-frequented rooms
- Replace incandescent bulbs with LED alternatives: 75-80% less energy for same brightness
- Utilize natural lighting during daylight hours to reduce daytime lighting needs
- Use task lighting instead of full-room illumination to save energy and reduce costs
- Estimated savings: $10-20/month on lighting costs alone

SMART THERMOSTAT STRATEGIES:
- Smart thermostats can reduce heating/cooling costs by 10-23% annually (up to $200/year)
- Recommended setpoints: 68°F in winter, 78°F in summer for optimal comfort and savings
- Utilize automated schedules: Lower/raise temps when away or sleeping
- Geofencing feature: Automatically adjusts temps based on your location
- Estimated savings: $15-50/month depending on climate and previous habits

LAUNDRY OPTIMIZATION:
- Wash clothes in cold water: Saves 80-90% of per-load washing energy
- Cold water is effective for 90% of laundry types and preserves clothing better
- Run full loads only: Partial loads use nearly the same energy for fewer clothes
- Use dryer's moisture sensor instead of timed drying for 20-30% energy savings
- Clean lint trap after every load to maintain efficiency and reduce fire risk
- Estimated savings: $15-25/month with proper techniques

APPLIANCE & MAINTENANCE BEST PRACTICES:
- Keep all appliances clean and well-maintained for optimal efficiency
- Refrigerator coils: Clean twice yearly to maintain cooling efficiency
- Washing machine filters: Clean regularly to prevent efficiency loss
- HVAC filters: Replace monthly for better airflow and reduced strain
- Estimated savings: 5-10% through improved appliance efficiency

HOME INSULATION & WEATHERIZATION:
- Seal air leaks around windows and doors with weatherstripping or caulk
- Air leaks account for 25-30% of heating/cooling energy loss
- Add insulation to attics and walls to reduce heating/cooling demands
- Attic insulation should be R-38 minimum in moderate climates, R-49+ in cold regions
- Estimated savings: $20-40/month depending on climate and current insulation

POWER MANAGEMENT & PHANTOM LOADS:
- Use power strips to turn off multiple devices at once
- Many electronics draw power even when "off" (phantom load): 5-10% of home electricity
- Unplug device chargers when not actively charging to eliminate phantom loads
- Smart plugs can automatically cut power to devices during idle periods
- Estimated savings: $5-15/month by eliminating phantom loads

ENERGY-EFFICIENT APPLIANCES:
- Replace old appliances with ENERGY STAR certified models
- ENERGY STAR appliances use 10-50% less energy than standard models
- Look for Energy Guide labels showing estimated annual operating costs
- New refrigerators, dishwashers, and washers offer significant long-term savings
- ROI typically achieved within 3-7 years through energy cost reductions

PASSIVE COOLING & SHADING STRATEGIES:
- Plant trees or install awnings to shade home during summer months
- Strategic shading can reduce cooling costs by 20-25% in summer
- Close blinds/curtains on sunny sides during hot days to reduce solar heat gain
- Use ceiling fans to circulate air and make rooms feel 4-8°F cooler
- Ceiling fans cost $0.01-0.03 per hour to operate vs. AC cooling
- Estimated savings: $20-50/month during cooling season with smart shading

WATER HEATING EFFICIENCY:
- Reduce hot water usage with low-flow showerheads and faucets
- Hot water heating accounts for 15-25% of home energy use
- Insulate hot water heater and pipes to reduce heat loss
- Set water heater temperature to 120°F for safety and efficiency
- Estimated savings: $10-20/month with smart hot water management
