Device-Specific Best Practices for Energy Optimization

Large devices like electric vehicles, washing machines, and dishwashers often support delayed start or timer functions. This creates opportunities for significant cost savings by strategically scheduling them during off-peak electricity pricing hours or periods of high solar generation. Smart scheduling can reduce device operating costs by 20-40%.

ELECTRIC VEHICLE (EV) CHARGING STRATEGY:
- Optimal charging windows: 11 PM to 6 AM (typical off-peak rates) or during peak solar generation (10 AM - 3 PM)
- Use Level 2 (240V) charging at home for 3-5x faster charging than Level 1
- Ideal daily strategy: Charge to 80% during off-peak hours; top off to 100% before needed
- Solar homes: Prioritize charging 11 AM - 2 PM when solar irradiance is highest
- Estimated savings: $40-80/month by shifting EV charging to off-peak hours
- Benefit from time-of-use (TOU) rates and demand charge reductions

WASHING MACHINE & DRYER OPTIMIZATION:
- Energy tip: Run full loads only—this is the single largest factor (40% efficiency gain)
- Water temperature: Cold water saves 80-90% of washing energy; suitable for 90% of loads
- Dryer usage: Moisture sensor mode reduces energy by 20-30% vs. timed drying
- Maintenance: Clean lint trap after every load to maintain efficiency and reduce fire risk
- Optimal scheduling: Run during off-peak hours, especially if you have solar (midday loads)
- Estimated savings: $15-25/month with optimized scheduling and techniques

DISHWASHER BEST PRACTICES:
- Only run when completely full; partial loads waste 20-30% more energy per dish
- Eco-mode or energy-saving setting reduces energy and water by 15-25%
- Skip heated dry cycle: Air dry or crack door after cycle; saves 10-15% per wash
- Optimal timing: Run during off-peak windows or high solar generation periods
- Estimated savings: $5-10/month with smart scheduling

WATER HEATER MANAGEMENT:
- Temperature setting: 120°F provides optimal balance between efficiency and comfort
- Insulate hot water pipes to reduce transmission losses by 10-15%
- Timer strategy: Turn off during peak hours or when away for extended periods
- Annual maintenance: Drain sediment to maintain efficiency and extend unit lifespan
- Estimated savings: $10-20/month with proper management

HVAC SYSTEM MAINTENANCE:
- Air filter changes: Monthly maintenance improves airflow and reduces compressor strain by 5-10%
- Outdoor condenser coils: Annual cleaning removes dust buildup, improving efficiency by 5%
- Programmable thermostat: Automated setbacks can save 10-15% on annual heating/cooling
- Ductwork sealing: Reduces conditioned air loss by 10-20%

POOL AND SPA EQUIPMENT:
- Pool pump scheduling: Run during off-peak hours to reduce electricity costs by 40-60%
- Variable-speed pumps: Use 50-90% less energy than single-speed models
- Pool covers: Reduce heating needs by 50-75% by minimizing evaporation
- Chemical balance: Proper maintenance reduces filtration needs and extends equipment life
- Estimated savings: $30-100/month depending on pool size and climate

UNIVERSAL DEVICE OPTIMIZATION PRINCIPLES:
1. Schedule flexible loads during off-peak pricing windows
2. Coordinate with solar generation when available
3. Avoid coincident peaks (multiple devices running simultaneously)
4. Regular maintenance extends device lifespan and maintains efficiency
5. Use smart home automation to enforce optimal scheduling automatically
