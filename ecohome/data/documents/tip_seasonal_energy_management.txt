Seasonal Energy Management Guide for Year-Round Optimization

WINTER ENERGY OPTIMIZATION (December - February):
- Pre-heat using cheaper off-peak rates in early morning before temperature drops
- Winter setpoint strategy: 68°F during occupied hours, 62-65°F during sleep/away
- Seal drafts around windows and doors; weather-stripping prevents heat loss (5-10% savings)
- Use ceiling fans on reverse setting (low speed) to push warm air down from ceiling
- Winter heating accounts for 40-50% of annual energy use; optimization critical for savings
- Strategy: Pre-heat before peak rates begin, maintain with thermostat coasting
- Estimated savings: 15-25% on heating costs with smart temperature management

SUMMER ENERGY OPTIMIZATION (June - August):
- Pre-cool home during early morning off-peak hours before heat builds
- Summer setpoint strategy: 78°F during occupied hours, cooler if using AC during peaks
- Close blinds and curtains on sunny sides during daytime to reduce solar heat gain
- Enable fan-only circulation overnight when outdoor temperatures drop below 70°F
- Use ceiling fans to create air circulation; feels 4-8°F cooler without lowering thermostat
- Raise thermostat temperature 2-4°F when fans are running for additional savings
- Summer cooling accounts for 30-40% of annual energy; peak-rate optimization most critical
- Estimated savings: 20-30% on cooling costs with strategic management

SHOULDER SEASONS STRATEGIES (March-May, September-November):
- Capitalize on free cooling/heating by opening windows when outdoor temp is 16-22°C (60-72°F)
- Monitor weather forecasts for mild temperature windows
- Use natural ventilation to reduce HVAC operation during shoulder seasons
- Potential savings: 40-60% HVAC reduction during optimal weather periods
- Close windows/doors when outdoor temp drifts outside comfort range
- Reduces electricity consumption 25-35% in shoulder months vs. summer/winter

EV CHARGING SEASONAL ADJUSTMENTS:
- Winter: Start EV charging later (post-midnight) to avoid heating conflicts with HVAC
- Cold weather EV preheating uses significant electricity; coordinate with off-peak periods
- Summer: Schedule EV charging for midday solar generation or early morning off-peak hours
- Avoid simultaneous EV charging and AC cooling during peak-rate hours
- Estimated seasonal variation: 20-30% more charging energy required in winter vs. summer

UTILITY RATE CHANGES & SEASONAL OPTIMIZATION:
- Review your utility tariff changes each season; many utilities shift peak windows
- Some utilities implement different rate schedules seasonally
- Winter rates may differ significantly from summer time-of-use pricing
- Adjust load-shifting strategy to match new rate structure each season
- Common pattern: Longer peak windows in summer (5 PM - 9 PM vs 4 PM - 8 PM in winter)

DEVICE-SPECIFIC SEASONAL OPTIMIZATION:
- Water heater: May require less heating in summer; consider timer shutdown
- Pool pumps: Run longer schedules during summer for maintenance, off-peak in winter
- Dishwasher/laundry: Shift from winter afternoon scheduling to summer midday solar hours
- Hot water usage increases in winter (showers); decrease in summer
- Estimated seasonal consumption variation: 15-25% depending on device type

HVAC MAINTENANCE SEASONAL SCHEDULE:
- Spring: Clean AC condenser coils before cooling season begins
- Summer: Check HVAC operation weekly; monitor for efficiency decline
- Fall: Service furnace and heat pump before winter heating demand
- Winter: Replace air filters monthly; clean filters reduce heating energy by 5-10%
- Regular seasonal maintenance improves efficiency 10-15% vs. neglected systems

DEMAND RESPONSE PEAK PLANNING:
- Many utilities offer demand response programs with different peaks by season
- Summer peaks typically: 5 PM - 9 PM during high cooling demand
- Winter peaks may shift earlier (4 PM - 8 PM) depending on region
- Plan battery discharge, EV charging, and appliance use around seasonal peak windows
- Participating in demand response can reduce peak-hour charges by 10-20%
